Configure Windows Update

Open settings app , Select "Update & security" -> "Windows Update".
In this interface, there're some important button under Update status section.

They're:
  • Change active hours
  • Restart options
  • Advanced options

Restart options

Turn on "Show more notifications".

Once you've done it, click the "←" button on title bar to back to privous interface.
This will give you a chance to decline computer restart while Windows Update if you have some documents or programs haven't save.
Of course, this will only work when you're not using full screen applications,
otherwise, you still not able to be notified and may timeout to response and get a restart without saving your contents.
You can search "Windows full screen exclusive mode" to get more information.

Advanced options

Choose how updates are installed

Select the checkbox of
"Give me updates for other Microsoft products when I update Windows".
This will allow Windows Update to install many other Microsoft products updates like it says,
The reason you should enable it is security.
Latest security of Microsoft products will delever to you soon once they're available and you don't need to worry about them.

Choose when updates are installed

I would not recommended the "Current Branch" for anyone,
this can bring you more restart request from Windows Update.
But if you get interested in latest Windows feature just like me, during the redstone update,
turn it to "Current Branch" and be excited.
Otherwize, use "Current Branch for Business".
You'll still get security updates, but feature updates will come more lately until they're stable enough.

Pause Updates

I understand many people hate Windows Update
and hope their fantastic Windows XP can up and running forever, without updates.
As a programmer, I would say, hey, you know nothing about power.
Lack latest feature update won't hurt you,
but when lacking security updates, the hackers can steal anything from you
even if you're simply connect your device to internet and do nothing else.
Don't turn this off, use it only if you're facing a pain can't reboot for hours.
You can turn it off while gaming or broadcast, but turn it back on once you're done.

EDIT: After this suggest wrote, I received some report shows Microsoft force a Windows 10 upgrade even if this option turned off.
This is a lie. It says it will pause for 30 days but it still did a dangerous upgrade and may damage running service or our data.
To fight back, here is two script for alternative. And these are faster than settings app click-click-click.
  • WindowsUpdateDisable.cmd
  • WindowsUpdateEnable.cmd
Right click the script and choose "Run as Administrator".
Even I did a stop Windows Update service request, a system reboot may still required to work properly.

Choose how updates are delivered

I left this options to off.
This options can speed up your Updates download speed,
but as the price that Microsoft not telling you explicit,
Windows will keep a cache on your system drive in order to share them to others,
and downcrase your hard drive performance while others need updates from you.
On another hand, keep extra cache files on SSD drives may reduce SSD life cycle.
I don't know, up to your manufacturers.
Make a decision according to your faith.
